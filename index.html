Welcome!!!

Why get into motorsport?

Mixing stunning speed with strategy takes physical strength and agility, plus a fair degree of bravery.

Who is it for?

If you like fast paced activities that get your heart pumping and your brain working, this is for you!

Is there a cheap option?

Some motor clubs run grassroots events that you can enter for a small fee using your standard road car.

What if I want a proper workout?

Trial biking is the most physically demanding motorsport, though all fast racing improves your core strength.

Can I take it to another level?

Competition licences/ applications are available for those wanting to seriously race cars or bikes.

Is there a disability option?

There are disability options within rallying, karting and motorcycling.

Is there a family option?

Go karting and motocross are safe activities for all the family to enjoy.

So how do I take part?

CAUTION!!!

Competition cautions are nothing new to racing. When the Camping World Truck Series was first formed, the schedule was comprised of short-tracks that didn't have large pit roads. In addition, the teams had small crews to save money. For several years a "halftime break" competition caution was thrown so teams could pit in tight pit roads, and have time to do their work, coming back out without losing any positions. The Xfinity Series also threw cautions so teams could take their time on very tight pit roads where crews would be in danger. The cars would return to the positions they had entering pit stops, after completing a slow, safe service.
